Map Distortions for the Statistics Canada Lambert (EPSG:3347)

The Statistics Canada Lambert (EPSG:3347) is a Lambert Conformal Conic map projection adopted by Statistics Canada. It uses two standard parallels, at 49° and 77° N, and the central meridian is located at 91.87° W. The interactive map below attempts to show the map distortions when selecting different values for the standard parallels. The distortions are quantified by showing the Tissot's Indicatrices at regular intervals. Recall that, in conformal map projections, the Tissot's ellipses only change in area, not shape, hence h = k. Because the distortions are rather difficult to asses on the map, a table showing the h and k scales values (and a dynamic bar) are displayed to the right of the map.
